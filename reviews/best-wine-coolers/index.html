Why and how to chill wine

Preservation

The majority of the wines you will find at your local store are best enjoyed within a few years of the label date. Is it realistic for the average wine drinker to store bottles of wine for more than a year? The short answer is no. If you are looking to store wines for maturing or expensive fine wine, itâ€™s best to invest in a professional grade wine cellar.

When storing wine for preservation (even if just a few days or weeks), the following best practices are recommended:

Cooled, not chilled:Â Ideal storage temperatures for white wine are between 49-55 Â°F and for red wine between 62-68 Â°F. Going lower in temperature can increase the risk of wine freezing, which can also push the cork out. Going higher can risk aging the wine more quickly, which may result in flat flavors.

Secure and steady:Â Keeping the temperatures consistent and not allowing a quick drop or rise is best for preserving your wines. Also, be sure to set the feet on the base of the wine fridge so it doesnâ€™t rock or shake the wine.

Lights out:Â Natural and artificial lights can be harmful to prolonged storage. Try to find a naturally dark area in your home for your wine fridge.

Humidity:Â For those who store wine long term, they need to ensure the cork doesnâ€™t try and spoil the wine. Â As long as the room has a humidity between 50-80% (which most homes do) and you store your wine on its side, this wonâ€™t be a problem for your short term storage.

Proper storage temps for red and white wine

It is important to understand that storing and serving temperatures are not the same. White wines store best between 49-55 Â°F and red wine between 62-68 Â°F. Most experts agree that if you are storing your wine in a dual zone wine fridge, you should take your bottle out of the wine fridge about 30 minutes prior to serving in order to bring it to optimal temperatures.

Place the red on the table or in a decanter to warm up slightly and the white should go in a bucket of ice to cool slightly. This best practice will also allow the wine flavor to develop as you drink the bottle.

If you are using a single zone wine cooler, it is recommended to store both red and whites at 55 Â°F. Reds can still go right to the table to serve but your white wine should probably spend about 30 minutes in the kitchen fridge to be brought down to serving temperature before placing it in a bucket of ice and pouring.

Some may ask why they canâ€™t store wine in their normal refrigerator. In doing our research, we were surprised to find the lengthy list of reasons why other than simply lack of storage space. We found that fridges donâ€™t have the humidity control thatâ€™s best for wine storage. Additionally, they vibrate when running, have a quick drop of temperature and lower internal temperatures.
