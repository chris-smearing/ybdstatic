How are self-monitoring systems different?

There are three major differences between a self-monitoring system and a monitored security system, and itâ€™s important to consider what is best for you and your family.

The first difference is that a traditional monitored system has alarms and signals that will alert the authorities if something happens. On a self-monitored system, no one else is watching your cameras, and the police or fire department are not alerted if something happens when youâ€™re not paying attention to notifications.

The second difference between the two will be the hardware included. Self-monitored systems all watch for motion, but monitored systems typically come with special smoke detectors, open-door alarms, window-opening alerts and a siren. You can assemble similar functionality with a DIY self-monitored system, but making sure it all works together is on you.

Finally, a monitored system will almost always use a telephone hard line or cell phone tower to communicate with a security company, while self-monitored systems rely on your home internet connection and Wi-Fi to update you if something happens. If your Wi-Fi is on the fritz, a self-monitoring wireless camera wonâ€™t work. Hereâ€™s a quick summary.

Monitored security system:

  • Connected to a police station and fire department
  • Special hardware for smoke and carbon dioxide detectors
  • Open window alarms
  • Hub station with an alarm
  • Professional installation

Self-monitored system:

  • Connects to your smartphone and computer
  • Alerts you only to motion per your custom settings
  • Adding other alert hardware can be tricky
  • Relies on your home internet connection
  • Subscription fees still a thing, but with different features for your money

A great option for renters or an alternative for homeowners is self-monitored systems. Self-monitored cameras still come with motion alerts, link normally to your phone and send messages based on your preferences.
